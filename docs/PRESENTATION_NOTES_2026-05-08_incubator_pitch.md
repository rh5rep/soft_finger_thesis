# Incubator Pitch Notes

## Primary Asset

- `docs/presentation_assets/incubator_pitch_deck_2026-05-08.html`

## What Changed In This Pass

- the visual system was simplified into a lighter, higher-contrast presentation with larger type and cleaner spacing
- slide titles and body copy were shortened to remove meta language and make the deck read more like a pitch
- the opening slide was reduced to one core thesis, one beachhead, and one clear round objective
- image panels were resized and the reference list was split across two slides to avoid cramped layouts
- substantive claims still carry explicit source references, while speculative product statements remain clearly bounded

## Positioning Used

- The deck positions the concept as a `preclinical variable-stiffness finger rehab platform`.
- The thesis-aligned product wedge is a `single-finger / distal / MCP-adjacent` system rather than a full glove.
- `Stroke rehabilitation` is presented as the clearest first commercial and validation-facing entry point.
- `Parkinson's disease` is presented as the strongest adjacent framing for `repetitive finger motion` and `task-level measurement`.
- The deck does **not** claim clinical efficacy, reimbursement readiness, or product-market fit.

## Evidence Rules Used

1. If a statement is burden, prevalence, or rehab-literature specific, cite an external source.
2. If a statement compares device-category clusters, present it as `qualitative synthesis`.
3. If a statement recommends a product or go-to-market direction, keep the wording concise and avoid presenting it as established fact unless directly supported.
4. If a statement describes current project status, it can rely on `repo-local evidence`.

## Current Claim Boundaries

### Source-backed

- stroke and Parkinson's burden framing
- stroke upper-limb rehab bottleneck framing
- finger tapping as a clinically meaningful and measurable task
- near-adjacent MCP / index-finger exoskeleton precedent
- remote or wearable measurement precedent for Parkinson finger tasks

### Inference / hypothesis

- that a variable-stiffness single-finger platform occupies a commercially valuable middle ground
- that clinic-guided training should be the first go-to-market step
- that the eventual company should expand from assistance into data-enabled workflow products

### Repo-backed local evidence

- current modeling and simulation base
- current thesis scope discipline
- current literature-map quality and memo structure

## Why This Version Is Better

- It is easier to read quickly in a live presentation.
- It frames the concept as a focused company wedge rather than a literature summary.
- It keeps the claims conservative without turning the deck into commentary about itself.
