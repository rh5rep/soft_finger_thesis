## What These Papers Let You Say Safely

- Parkinson-relevant finger movement impairment is not just generic weakness or range-of-motion loss.
- Finger tapping is a legitimate and information-rich task for studying Parkinson-related motor behavior.
- The direct Parkinson finger literature is much stronger for `assessment and quantification` than for `assistive exoskeleton hardware`.
- A proposal can therefore justify a simplified finger system by combining `task-definition papers` with `near-adjacent hardware papers`.

## What Not To Overclaim From This Group

- These papers do not by themselves justify a specific actuator architecture.
- Assessment papers should not be cited as if they already solve the assistance or stabilization problem.
- Finger tapping is a strong task-definition anchor, but it is not itself evidence that a full wearable clinical device should be built in this thesis.
