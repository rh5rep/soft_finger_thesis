# Useful Parkinson Rehabilitation Papers

## Purpose

This is not a comprehensive Parkinson bibliography. It is a `usefulness-filtered` list of papers that should help with thesis framing, symptom targeting, outcomes, and realistic device expectations.

## Tier 1: Read Soon

### 1. Bologna et al. 2020

- `Evolving concepts on bradykinesia`
- Brain
- DOI: `10.1093/brain/awz344`
- PubMed: [31834375](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31834375/)
- PMC: [open access](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8205506/)

Why this is useful:

- best paper so far for understanding what `bradykinesia` actually includes
- connects slowness, reduced amplitude, and `sequence effect`
- directly useful for deciding what Parkinson-relevant movement problem a finger thesis might target

### 2. Capato et al. 2023

- `Clinical assessment of upper limb impairments and functional capacity in Parkinson's disease: a systematic review`
- Arquivos de Neuro-Psiquiatria
- DOI: `10.1055/s-0043-1772769`
- PubMed: [37899049](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37899049/)
- PMC: [open access](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10689111/)

Why this is useful:

- very useful bridge paper for upper-limb and hand function in PD
- shows which upper-limb outcome measures are commonly used
- highlights the shortage of standardized functional upper-limb assessment in PD

### 3. Raciti et al. 2025

- `Exploring the Impact of Robotic Hand Rehabilitation on Functional Recovery in Parkinson’s Disease: A Randomized Controlled Trial`
- Brain Sciences
- DOI: `10.3390/brainsci15060644`
- PubMed: [40563814](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40563814/)
- PMC: [open access](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12190367/)

Why this is useful:

- one of the most directly relevant PD hand-rehab papers in the current queue
- useful for `hand-focused robotic rehab in PD`
- especially relevant for questions about cognition versus motor outcomes and how to interpret mixed evidence

### 4. Raciti et al. 2022

- `Improving Upper Extremity Bradykinesia in Parkinson’s Disease: A Randomized Clinical Trial on the Use of Gravity-Supporting Exoskeletons`
- Journal of Clinical Medicine
- DOI: `10.3390/jcm11092543`
- DOI link: [10.3390/jcm11092543](https://doi.org/10.3390/jcm11092543)

Why this is useful:

- good Parkinson-specific upper-limb rehab positioning paper
- not finger-specific, but useful for seeing how PD rehab trials frame dexterity, repetitive training, and outcome measures

## Tier 2: High-Value Broad Context

### 5. Tao et al. 2024

- `The role of robot-assisted training on rehabilitation outcomes in Parkinson's disease: a systematic review and meta-analysis`
- Disability and Rehabilitation
- DOI: `10.1080/09638288.2023.2266178`
- PubMed: [37818694](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37818694/)

Why this is useful:

- gives a broader map of robot-assisted rehabilitation in PD
- useful for seeing where the evidence is actually strongest, which is often gait and mobility rather than hand function

### 6. Wu et al. 2025

- `Effect of robot-assisted rehabilitation of patients with Parkinson's disease: A meta-analysis`
- Clinical Rehabilitation
- DOI: `10.1177/02692155251355089`
- PubMed: [40629925](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40629925/)

Why this is useful:

- current meta-analysis across `22 studies`
- helpful for not overgeneralizing from one or two device papers
- useful as a check against overly optimistic single-study conclusions

### 7. Ernst et al. 2024

- `Physical exercise for people with Parkinson's disease: a systematic review and network meta-analysis`
- Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews
- DOI: `10.1002/14651858.CD013856.pub3`
- PubMed: [38588457](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38588457/)

Why this is useful:

- exercise and rehabilitation logic matter for PD framing even if the thesis is device-focused
- useful for understanding how broader rehab thinking compares with device-specific studies

## Tier 3: Useful Comparison Buckets

### 8. Zhang et al. 2024

- `The Efficacy of Wearable Cueing Devices on Gait and Motor Function in Parkinson Disease: A Systematic Review and Meta-analysis of Randomized Controlled Trials`
- Archives of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation
- DOI: `10.1016/j.apmr.2023.07.007`
- PubMed: [37532166](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37532166/)

Why this is useful:

- good comparison bucket for non-invasive assistive logic
- useful for understanding `cueing` as a separate intervention strategy from robotic assistance

### 9. Nascimento et al. 2024

- `Walking training with auditory cueing improves walking speed more than walking training alone in ambulatory people with Parkinson's disease: a systematic review`
- Journal of Physiotherapy
- DOI: `10.1016/j.jphys.2024.06.004`
- PubMed: [38897907](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38897907/)

Why this is useful:

- another useful comparison paper showing that rhythmic/cue-based support is a major rehabilitation logic in PD
- good for not collapsing all assistive/control approaches into robotics

## Current Reading Recommendation

If the goal is `useful now`, read in this order:

1. `Bologna 2020`
2. `Capato 2023`
3. `Raciti 2025`
4. `Raciti 2022`
5. `Tao 2024`
6. `Wu 2025`

## Current Thesis Relevance

The most useful lessons these papers seem likely to provide are:

- what bradykinesia actually means at the movement level
- what upper-limb outcomes in PD are commonly measured
- where hand rehab sits within broader PD rehabilitation
- how strong or weak the robotic-rehab evidence really is
- where the biggest gap remains: `finger-specific, mechanistically interpretable, simplified assistance/stabilization work`
