# Parkinson's Learning Resources

## Goal

Build enough Parkinson's background to read the rehab and device literature intelligently without drifting into an unfocused medical deep dive.

## Recommended Learning Path

### 1. Fast Orientation

Read these first:

- [Parkinson's Foundation: Understanding Parkinson's](https://www.parkinson.org/understanding-parkinsons)
- [Parkinson's Foundation: What is Parkinson's?](https://www.parkinson.org/understanding-parkinsons/what-is-parkinsons)
- [NIEHS: Parkinson's Disease](https://www.niehs.nih.gov/health/topics/conditions/parkinson)
- [NHS Wales overview: Parkinson's disease](https://111.wales.nhs.uk/encyclopaedia/p/article/parkinsonsdisease)

These are good for:

- symptoms
- causes and risk framing
- treatment overview
- who is affected

### 2. Core Review Papers

Use these to move from public-health-level summary to academic understanding:

- `Kalia and Lang, 2015`
  - `Parkinson's Disease`
  - Lancet
  - DOI: `10.1016/S0140-6736(14)61393-3`
- `Poewe et al., 2017`
  - `Parkinson disease`
  - Nature Reviews Disease Primers
  - DOI: `10.1038/nrdp.2017.13`
- `Bologna et al., 2020`
  - `Evolving concepts on bradykinesia`
  - Brain
  - PMC: [open access link](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8205506/)
- `Chaudhuri et al., 2006`
  - `Non-motor symptoms of Parkinson's disease: diagnosis and management`
  - Lancet Neurology
  - DOI: `10.1016/S1474-4422(06)70373-8`

These are good for:

- disease mechanisms
- symptom domains
- bradykinesia specifically
- why non-motor symptoms matter

### 3. Rehabilitation And Exercise Context

- [Parkinson's Foundation: Exercise and PD](https://www.parkinson.org/index.php/library/fact-sheets/exercise)
- `Raciti et al. 2022`
  - PD upper-limb exoskeleton rehab context
  - DOI: `10.3390/jcm11092543`
- `Raciti et al. 2025`
  - PD robotic hand rehab context
  - DOI: `10.3390/brainsci15060644`

These are good for:

- how Parkinson rehab is framed clinically
- why repetitive task-oriented training matters
- where hand-focused robotic rehab sits within the broader field

### 4. Videos And Webinars

Use these when reading starts feeling too abstract:

- [Parkinson's Foundation Expert Briefing Webinars](https://www.parkinson.org/resources-support/online-education/expert-briefings-webinars)
- [Michael J. Fox Foundation Webinars](https://www.michaeljfox.org/webinars)
- [Davis Phinney Foundation Parkinson's Webinar Series](https://davisphinneyfoundation.org/webinars/)

These are useful for:

- hearing clinicians explain symptom impact in plain language
- understanding patient-facing priorities
- getting a better sense of real-life burden beyond metrics and tables

### 5. News And Local Research Context

Use these for broad context around Parkinson's research activity, local stakeholders, and possible conversation framing. Do not use them as primary evidence for thesis claims unless the underlying project, publications, or clinical evidence are read directly.

- [Draupnir Bio: Groundbreaking Danish research project launches to cure Parkinson's disease](https://draupnir.bio/news/groundbreaking-danish-research-project-launches-to-cure-parkinsons-disease)
  - Keep as Danish Parkinson's research ecosystem context.
  - Relevance to thesis is indirect: useful for understanding local research momentum and possible background framing, not for the finger-actuator model or validation strategy.
  - If cited later, trace claims back to peer-reviewed papers, project documentation, or primary clinical sources.

## What To Capture While Learning

For each resource, write down:

- what symptom or problem it emphasizes
- whether it is about `assessment`, `treatment`, `rehab`, or `daily living`
- what outcomes matter most
- whether hand/finger dysfunction appears directly or only indirectly
- any phrase that helps explain why your thesis matters
