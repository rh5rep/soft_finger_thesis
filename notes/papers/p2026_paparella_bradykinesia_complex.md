## Source-Verified Summary

- This paper extends the "bradykinesia complex" framing into a large finger-tapping kinematic study.
- It analyzes finger tapping in 350 individuals and explicitly separates bradykinesia, hypokinesia, dysrhythmia, and sequence effect.
- One of its strongest thesis-relevant implications is that the authors argue "bradykinesia and associated features" may be a better label than the narrower current clinical definition.
- For the thesis, this supports a more careful task-framing section and makes it harder to talk about Parkinson finger tapping as if one motor abnormality captures everything.

## Model / Mechanism / Validation Details

- System type:
- Actuation type: none
- Model type: finger-tapping kinematic analysis with ROC, clustering, and Bayesian combination analysis
- Validation setup: 350 individuals, including 192 PD patients OFF medication and 158 healthy controls, plus an ON-medication subgroup
- Main metrics: presence and combinations of bradykinesia, hypokinesia, dysrhythmia, and sequence effect; probabilities associated with feature combinations

## What Matters For This Thesis

- This is one of the strongest bridge papers between disease framing and direct finger-tapping task analysis.
- It supports discussing Parkinson finger tapping in terms of separable abnormality families, not only a single performance score.
- It also warns against over-valuing sequence effect on its own, because the paper reports that isolated sequence effect can appear in healthy elderly individuals and may be nonspecific.
